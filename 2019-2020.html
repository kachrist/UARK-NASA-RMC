2019-2020

Design Philosophy

The team goal for this year is to autonomously mining 1kg of gravel while maintaining the strides in weight reduction achieved in the previous. To achieve this, the team has decided to

1) Optimise the excavation system by identifying the optimal excavation method for quickly digging through the regolith and collecting as much icy regolith as efficient.

2) Alter the mobility system to meet competition requirements and improve the previous years' design.

3) Design for sensor integration while planning and developing the programs required to use ROS for autonomous navigation across the arena and excavation, collection, and dumping of the icy regolith at the appropriate locations.

Team Structure

Six sub-teams consisting of all three disciplines focused on the design and fabrication of the robot’s mobility system, the excavation system, autonomy systems, as well as developing and building a testing bed for the robot’s physical systems, writing the systems engineering report, and cultivating and coordinating outreach opportunities.

Mobility

The Mobility team focused on optimizing and shrinking the chassis designed in 2018-2019, allowing the team to maintain the weight reduction.

Excavation

The Excavation team focused on developing a creative and effective excavation system through prototyping and physical testing.

Testing

The Testing team tackled the problem of finding a location to safely perform physical tests for design evaluation.

Computer Systems

The Computer Systems team is working on sensor selection, controls programming, as well as the design and implimentation of autonomy.

Writing

The Writing team has a member inbedded in each subteam assisting and documenting team progress to develop the best systems engineering report possible.

Outreach

The Outreach team worked on building long term contacts with local schools and organizations.
