Facilites

The University of Arkansas has provided a robotoic lab for the team to meet and access to 3D printers and a machine shop for constructing the team's robot.

Robotics Lab

Our Robots Lab was recently upgraded to help facilitate the needs of our growing team. We have three computer stations to allow multiple students to research, code, design, and run complex FEA (Finite Element Analysis).

3D Print Lab

Directly nextdoor to our lab are 4 high quality _(brand/name)_ 3D Printers allowing our team members to rapidly prototype their designs in a matter of hours. Our team has also found that these 3D prints are strong enough to use in a wide range of applications on the final robot ranging from our wheels to electrical boxes. Any brightly colored parts that you see on our robots are likely a 3D print.

❮ ❯

Machine Shop

Our team also has access to full machine shop just downstairs kitted out with both manual and CNC mills and lathes along with bandsaws, belt sanders, a laser cutter and much more. This allows our team to manufacture strong and complex parts while maintaining high precision as well as improve design by helping students learn to consider manufacturability.

Testing Facilites

Our team has recently acquired a storage container that we are working hard to fix up and clear out in order to allow us to be able to test our excavation and mobility systems in crushed limestone, a BP-1 simulant. The team is on track to have it finished in time for testing the 2020 robot.
